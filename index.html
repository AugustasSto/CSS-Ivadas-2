Cat

The earliest known indication for the taming of an African wildcat (F. lybica) was excavated close by a human Neolithic grave in Shillourokambos, southern Cyprus, dating to about 7500–7200 BC.

Lifespan: 2 – 16 years (In the wild)
Gestation period: 58 – 67 days
Daily sleep: 12 – 16 hours
Mass: 3.6 – 4.5 kg (Adult)

The cat is a domestic species of small carnivorous mammal. It is the only domesticated species in the family Felidae and is often referred to as the domestic cat to distinguish it from the wild members of the family.
